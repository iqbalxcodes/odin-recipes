Ayam Suwir Recipe

is Indonesian shredded spicy chicken. Every weekend I try to make big quantity dishes so that I can have them for a few days, especially now that I donâ€™t have much time to cook during the day. I paired this dish with my Tempe Orek (Indonesian crisp sweet&sour Tempe), and it felt just like home. Because I made it in such a big quantity, feel free to adjust according to your servings. Hope you like this recipe and happy cooking!

Ingredients

  • 1.6 kg chicken breast (about 8 chicken breasts)
  • 6 big chilles
  • 3 small chillies
  • 4 shallot
  • 3 garlic
  • 4 tomato
  • 4 lemon grass, crush the bottom part to release the aroma
  • 6 lime leaves
  • 2 cm galangal, crushed
  • 100 gr coconut milk, or 3 tbsp coconut powder + 1 cup of water
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 3 tbsp sugar
  • 1/3 cup of water
  • vegetable oil

Steps

  1. In a deep pot, boil chicken breast for about 12-20 minutes or until it's cooked. Place the chicken breast on a plate to cool down. You can use the left over water for soup if you want to. Shred chicken breast once it's cooled down, using hand or fork
  2. In a wok/cooking pan, heat up about 1 cup of vegetable oil on medium-high. Fry the chillies, shallots, and garlics for about 5 minutes until it's half cooked. Take it out and blend. Leave some small chunks if you like
  3. In a wok/cooking pan, heat up a few spoons of vegetable oil on medium-high. Stir blended ingredients, lemon grass, lime leaves, and galangal for 2-3 minutes until it's fragrant
  4. throw in the shredded chicken, mix until it's all covered with the sauce
  5. Add in coconut milk, salt and sugar. Stir another 5 minutes on medium until coconut milk is absorbed and ready to serve
